Decision flow

Office profile vs practitioner profile formula

Use this structure:
[main office identity] + [which provider pages are truly public-facing] + [how patients or buyers actually book] + [what should stay live vs what should be cleaned up].

Rule: the office listing should explain the brand and booking path clearly, while practitioner pages should only exist when they reflect real public-facing providers rather than leftover clutter.
  • Best for owners who do not know whether they have a legitimate practitioner setup or a duplicate-profile problem.
  • Use this before deciding on removal, merge, or rewrite actions.
Office summary

Main office clarification template

Template:
This Google Business Profile represents the primary office for [Business Name]. Patients/customers book through this location for [core services], and this page should remain the main brand-facing profile for the business.

Any practitioner pages should support the real provider structure without replacing the primary office identity.
  • Best for dental offices, clinics, med spas, and therapy groups with one clear front-door brand.
  • Useful when the office listing has become vague because staff tried to optimize too many provider pages at once.
Provider page setup

Active practitioner profile template

Template:
This profile represents [Practitioner Name], an active provider at [Office Name]. The practitioner sees patients/customers through the office and is publicly associated with [specialty/service focus].

Booking, office details, and supporting proof align with the main office setup so the practitioner page adds clarity instead of confusion.
  • Best when the practitioner really is a public-facing provider with a legitimate role.
  • Use this to keep the provider page tightly tied to the office structure.
Former provider cleanup

Departed practitioner cleanup template

Template:
The practitioner previously associated with this profile is no longer part of the current office setup. The office continues to operate at [current office details], but this specific provider page is outdated and may now create confusion for patients/customers.

Please review the profile and help align it with the current active provider structure.
  • Best for offices inheriting stale doctor, dentist, or therapist pages after staff turnover.
  • Useful before duplicate-cleanup or reinstatement work snowballs.
Same office, many providers

Multi-provider clarification template

Template:
This office includes multiple active providers. The main office profile should remain the primary business identity, while any provider profiles should clearly reflect individual practitioners without duplicating the office page's core role.

Please review the structure so the office profile stays strong and the provider pages remain accurate and non-conflicting.
  • Best for orthodontics, med spas, group practices, and specialty clinics with several named providers.
  • Use this when provider pages exist but the office page is losing clarity or authority.
Booking-path clarity

Office booking vs practitioner booking template

Template:
Customers book through [main office / central phone / office website], even when they are looking for a specific provider. The business wants the Google Business Profile structure to match that real booking path so the office and provider pages support each other instead of competing.

Please review the profile wording and structure with that booking path in mind.
  • Best when the office page and practitioner pages send buyers to conflicting contact paths.
  • Useful for improving both conversion clarity and profile consistency.
Verification + proof

Provider-proof checklist

Gather:
- Current office website pages showing the active provider roster
- Booking or contact pages demonstrating how patients/customers actually reach the office
- Licensing or professional details only when truly needed and publicly supportable
- Screenshots of stale provider pages, duplicate names, or old office references
- A short note explaining the current office-vs-provider structure

The goal is not dumping every document. The goal is making the active provider structure easy to understand.
  • Best when provider confusion overlaps with verification or duplicate-cleanup work.
  • Pairs well with the verification and duplicate-cleanup pages.
