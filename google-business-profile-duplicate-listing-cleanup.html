Google Business Profile duplicate listing cleanup guide

Treat this as a practical cleanup sequence, not a platform guarantee. The goal is to identify the one profile that should survive, remove contradictory duplicates cleanly, and make the business identity easy for Google and buyers to understand again.

Stage 1 • Inventory the mess

List every profile, location variant, and practitioner page first

  • Search the brand name, old DBA names, prior addresses, old phone numbers, and practitioner names in Google Maps.
  • Document each live or suppressed listing URL, the visible business name, phone, category, and address or service-area setup.
  • Flag which pages appear to represent the same business versus a real separate practitioner or second location.
  • Do not start random edits until you know the full duplicate set.
Stage 2 • Pick the canonical survivor

Decide which profile should keep the demand, reviews, and authority

  • Prefer the profile that matches the current real-world business setup, core category, and best proof trail.
  • Do not keep an old address-based profile alive just because it has age if the business no longer operates that way.
  • For practitioner-heavy businesses, confirm whether the extra profile is legitimately separate or should fold into the brand page.
  • Write down one sentence explaining why the chosen profile is the canonical listing.
Stage 3 • Clean public contradictions

Make the website and citation layer support the same story

  • Update the website footer, contact page, schema, and location or practitioner pages so they reflect the surviving setup.
  • Remove or rewrite pages that promote retired addresses, duplicate phone numbers, or inactive practitioner/location combinations.
  • Check top citations and social profiles for stale naming or location details that make the duplicate look legitimate.
  • If the business moved, explain the move cleanly instead of leaving both addresses active everywhere.
Stage 4 • Choose the cleanup action

Match the tactic to the duplicate type

  • Old location that no longer exists: prepare to mark closed or request removal with proof.
  • Practitioner/profile overlap: decide whether the practitioner page is truly eligible or should be removed or merged conceptually.
  • Service-area/storefront conflict: rewrite the business model and service-area explanation before requesting change.
  • Near-identical duplicate still live in Maps: prepare a short factual note explaining which profile is correct and why the other one creates confusion.
Stage 5 • Support-note prep

Draft one clean duplicate-cleanup explanation

  • State which listing is the current legitimate business profile.
  • State which listing appears to be the duplicate or retired variant.
  • Explain whether the issue came from an old move, practitioner confusion, prior marketer edits, or citation drift.
  • Reference the proof and public corrections already completed.
Simple duplicate-cleanup note:
The current legitimate Google Business Profile for this business is [canonical listing name/location].
The duplicate or retired listing is [duplicate name/location].
The duplicate appears to come from [old address / practitioner overlap / prior setup].
We have updated the website and core public details to match the current business setup and request cleanup or review of the duplicate listing.
Stage 6 • Post-cleanup stabilization

Do not stop at removal — strengthen the survivor

  • After the duplicate is removed or de-emphasized, review categories, services, photos, hours, and review flow on the surviving profile.
  • Track whether review count, ranking behavior, and branded searches are now consolidating around the right page.
  • Capture a short internal note about what caused the duplicate so the same setup is not recreated later.
  • Move into the optimization checklist or paid audit if the surviving profile is still weak on conversion and local relevance.
