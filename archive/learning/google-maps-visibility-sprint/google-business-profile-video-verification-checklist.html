Free Google Business Profile video verification checklist

Use these blocks to make the recording easier to review and easier to trust. The goal is not cinematic quality. The goal is one practical walkthrough that clearly proves the business exists, matches the profile, and actually operates the way the listing claims.

First principle

What the video must prove

The video should answer three questions clearly:
1. Is this a real business?
2. Does it operate the way the profile says it does?
3. Is the physical setup, signage, equipment, or practitioner context believable and current?

If the recording feels generic, disconnected from the listed category, or impossible to match to the public profile, redo it.
  • Best for owners who are overthinking the format but under-explaining the real setup.
  • Keep the proof factual and category-aligned instead of trying to sound persuasive.
Universal shot order

Use this simple one-take sequence

Recommended order:
1. Exterior sign, office entry, suite marker, or branded vehicle
2. Street view / building context that matches the listing setup
3. Workspace, front desk, treatment room, service equipment, or field tools
4. Proof of active operations: scheduling screen, work board, branded material, or service records context
5. Category-specific cue: dental chairs, med spa treatment setup, plumbing gear, detailing bay, etc.
6. Close with a short spoken explanation of how customers are actually served
  • Strong default for almost every local-service model.
  • Shorter, clearer sequences usually outperform long wandering tours.
Storefronts

Storefront video checklist

Show:
- Exterior signage or unit identification
- Door, lobby, waiting area, or front-desk context
- Posted hours or customer-facing entry cues when available
- Branded materials, staff area, or operating space tied to the real category
- A short explanation that customers visit this location during listed hours
  • Best for med spas, dentists, orthodontists, clinics, retail-facing service businesses, and detail shops.
  • Good when Google needs stronger location trust, not just legal paperwork.
Service-area businesses

Service-area video checklist

Show:
- Branded service vehicle if available
- Equipment, tools, inventory, uniforms, or job materials
- Dispatch / scheduling / route context that proves real field work
- Brief explanation that customers are served at their location, not as walk-ins
- Any matching website / phone / category cues that reinforce the same operating model
  • Best for plumbers, HVAC companies, locksmiths, cleaners, landscapers, roofers, pest control, and restoration teams.
  • Clarify the field-service model explicitly so the video does not look like a weak storefront attempt.
Practitioner-led businesses

Provider / office video checklist

Show:
- Office signage or suite context
- Front desk or patient-facing intake area
- Treatment room / provider setup tied to the listed specialty
- Provider materials, licenses, branded forms, or office identifiers when appropriate
- Short clarification of whether the profile is for the office brand, the practitioner, or both
  • Best for dentists, orthodontists, chiropractors, therapists, med spas, and multi-provider clinics.
  • Pair this with the practitioner profile library if the structure itself is still confusing.
Do not show this

Common video verification mistakes

Avoid:
- Long silent pans with no explanation
- Random close-ups that do not prove the business model
- Empty rooms with no category context
- Contradictory setup (for example acting like a storefront when it is really service-area)
- Old signage, former-provider materials, or outdated branding still visible
- A script that sounds like marketing copy instead of a factual walkthrough
  • Most failed recordings are not “too short” — they are too vague.
  • If old branding or former-provider residue is visible, fix that first.
Before you record

Five-minute prep checklist

Before recording:
- Confirm the profile category and operating model are accurate
- Remove obvious contradiction cues if possible
- Decide whether this is storefront, service-area, or practitioner-led
- Gather one or two category-specific proof items you can show naturally
- Practice the closing explanation once so the video stays short and clear
  • Useful when the owner keeps re-recording without changing the proof.
  • Prep matters more than fancy production quality.
Spoken script

Simple closing explanation

Template:
This is [Business Name]. We operate as a real [business type] serving customers in [city / region]. This video shows our [storefront / service setup / practitioner office], our working environment, and the materials that match the Google Business Profile listing so verification can be completed accurately.

Keep it calm, factual, and specific to the business model.
  • Best for owners who need wording help but do not want a long script.
  • Do not add claims the video cannot visually support.
If the first try failed

How to improve the second attempt

Fix the weakest proof gap, not just the delivery:
- If location trust was weak, add stronger exterior / suite / sign context
- If the business model was unclear, state storefront vs service-area explicitly
- If provider structure was messy, clean that first and use the practitioner profile page
- If duplicates or old profiles are involved, pair this with duplicate cleanup before retrying
- If suspension / reinstatement is already in play, use the reinstatement checklist before recording again
  • Use new clarity, not just another nearly identical walkthrough.
  • Escalate to the paid audit or DFY path once the listing is live and still underperforming.
