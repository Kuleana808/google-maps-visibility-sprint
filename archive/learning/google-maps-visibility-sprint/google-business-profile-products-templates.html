Universal products workflow

Core GBP product-card formula

Use this structure:
[Offer name] + [who it is for] + [core deliverable] + [light outcome cue].

Example:
New Patient Whitening Consult
For patients exploring cosmetic smile options. Includes a quick fit-check, treatment overview, and clear next-step guidance.

Rule: make the product card read like a packaged service outcome, not a SKU.
  • Best for businesses that need a clean first-pass product card before getting niche-specific.
  • Use this when the profile has blank product cards, vague labels, or mismatched service packaging.
Offer packaging

How to name one GBP product clearly

Formula:
[Problem / desired result] + [service type] + [light qualifier].

Examples:
- Emergency Drain Relief Visit
- Seasonal AC Tune-Up Package
- Smile Consultation Starter Visit
- Move-Out Cleaning Quote Package

If the name does not hint at the job-to-be-done, buyers skim past it.
  • Useful when the business has a real service offer but no buyer-friendly way to package it on the profile.
  • Pairs well with the GBP booking CTA library when the product card should drive a quote or consult request.

Decision rule

How to choose the right GBP product card

Use one card for the most common fast-buying service.
Use one card for the premium or higher-ticket consult.
Use one card for a seasonal, urgent, or promo-driven offer.
Keep the description focused on what the buyer gets next.

If the product card sounds like an internal service label, it will not help conversion.

How should a buyer use these templates?

Pick the closest niche example, rename the card around the actual buyer outcome, then tighten the description until it matches how the business really sells. The goal is not sounding clever. The goal is making the featured offer feel obvious and clickable.
