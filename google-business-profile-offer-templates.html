Universal offer workflow

Core GBP offer formula

Use this structure:
[Offer name] + [who it is for] + [core deliverable] + [time or urgency cue].

Examples:
- New Customer Drain Inspection for homeowners who want a fast first look before a bigger plumbing repair.
- Summer AC Tune-Up for homeowners who want cleaner cooling performance before peak heat hits.
- Smile Consultation Offer for patients comparing treatment options this month.

Rule: keep the offer outcome-specific and easy to claim. If it sounds like a vague ad, rewrite it.
  • Best for businesses that need a clean first-pass offer structure before getting niche-specific.
  • Use this when the profile has services listed but no clear reason for a buyer to act now.
New-customer intro offers

First-visit offer template

Formula:
[New customer / first visit] + [entry offer] + [how to claim].

Examples:
- First-time customers: request a same-week estimate and mention this profile for priority scheduling.
- New patient consult: submit your preferred day and we will confirm the closest opening.
- New client deep clean: ask for a quote this week and we will outline the best-fit package.

Best when the goal is lowering friction, not racing to the biggest discount.
  • Useful for operators who need a low-friction first step instead of a heavy price cut.
  • Pairs well with the booking CTA library when the offer needs a clearer request path.

Decision rule

How to choose the right GBP offer

Use a new-customer entry offer when trust is the main blocker.
Use an inspection or consult offer when the buyer needs diagnosis before commitment.
Use a seasonal or urgent offer when timing is the real conversion lever.
Use a maintenance-plan offer when retention matters as much as the first booking.

If the offer does not make the next step obvious, rewrite the claim path before publishing.

How should a buyer use these templates?

Pick the closest niche example, swap in the real service, delivery window, and claim path, then make sure the offer matches reality. The goal is not sounding promotional. The goal is giving the buyer a clean reason to choose a next step.
