Universal triage workflow

Core verification-response formula

Use this structure:
[Real business setup] + [how customers are served] + [what proof is available] + [direct request for review or next step].

Example:
This business operates as a legitimate local service business serving customers in [city/region]. We serve customers at [storefront / service area / office] and have attached proof of the business entity, location details, and service setup. Please review the verification materials and confirm the next step required to complete verification.

Rule: keep the explanation factual, short, and easy to verify.
  • Best for owners who need one stable structure before choosing a niche scenario.
  • Use this when the first draft is vague, emotional, or missing the actual operating details.
Documentation checklist

What to gather before verification

Common proof bundle:
- Government business registration or license
- Utility bill, lease, tax document, or other location proof when applicable
- Website, phone, and address details that match the profile
- Photos of storefront signage, office entrance, branded vehicle, equipment, or work context
- Short note clarifying whether the business is storefront, service-area, or practitioner-led
- Any prior verification notice, postcard issue, or review request reference

Do not flood the submission with random files. Use the smallest proof set that explains the setup clearly.
  • Useful when the operator is unsure whether the delay is caused by weak evidence or weak wording.
  • Pairs well with the suspension appeal page if verification confusion later becomes a policy-review problem.
Verification request summary

Base verification template

Template:
Hello Google Business Profile Support,

We are working to complete verification for [Business Name]. This is a legitimate [type of business] serving customers in [city/region]. The profile reflects the real business setup and public-facing contact details.

Attached are documents and supporting materials confirming the business entity and operating model, including [list your key proof items]. Please review the materials and let us know if any additional verification step is still required.

Thank you,
[Owner / manager name]
  • Best for owners who need a clean first-pass note instead of guessing how to phrase the request.
  • Keep it direct and do not claim proof you cannot actually provide.

Storefront proof template

Our business operates from a customer-facing location at [address]. Customers can visit during listed hours, and the profile reflects the real storefront setup.

Attached are documents confirming the address plus photos showing signage, entrance, and the operating location.

  • Best for med spas, dental offices, clinics, retail-facing service businesses, and detail shops.

Service-area businesses

Service-area verification template

Template:
Our business operates as a service-area business and travels to customers rather than relying on walk-in traffic at a retail storefront. The profile reflects our real service area and current business category.

Attached are documents confirming the business entity and evidence of field-service operations, such as branded vehicle, equipment, service records, or website and phone consistency.

Please review the materials and advise on any next step needed to complete verification.
  • Best for plumbers, HVAC companies, cleaners, locksmiths, landscapers, roofers, and other mobile operators.
  • Use this when hidden-address or field-service setups are causing ambiguity.
Video verification

Video walkthrough checklist + script

Show this sequence:
1. Exterior sign, office entrance, or service vehicle branding
2. Workspace, tools, equipment, or front-desk context
3. Proof that the listed business is actively operating at or from that setup
4. Any branded materials or job context that match the profile category

Short script:
This is [Business Name]. We operate from [location / service area] and serve customers in [city/region]. This video shows our signage, working setup, and business materials so the profile can be verified accurately.
  • Useful when postcard methods are unavailable or video review is the required next step.
  • Keep the walkthrough practical and aligned with the exact business model on the profile.
Postcard delays

Postcard follow-up template

Template:
We are following up on the verification process for [Business Name]. The business information on the profile is accurate, but the verification postcard has not resolved the setup yet.

Attached are supporting details confirming the business entity and operating address. Please advise whether the next step should be re-mailing the verification, switching methods, or reviewing the proof provided.
  • Best for operators stuck in repeated postcard delays without clear guidance.
  • Useful when the owner wants a calmer follow-up instead of restarting the whole setup blindly.
Address mismatches

Address-proof clarification template

Template:
We understand that the address and verification details must match the real operating setup. Attached are supporting documents showing the current business address and the ownership or occupancy relationship tied to that location.

If the profile should instead be configured as a service-area business or needs address-display changes, we are prepared to correct that immediately.

Please review the proof and confirm the cleanest next step for verification.
  • Useful when suite numbers, shared offices, relocations, or home-based operations created confusion.
  • Keep every document aligned with the live profile details where possible.
