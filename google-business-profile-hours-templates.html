Universal hours workflow

Core regular-hours formula

Use this structure:
[Open days + time range] + [how fast you respond] + [exception note if needed].

Examples:
- Open Monday-Friday, 8am-5pm. Same-day callbacks during business hours.
- Open daily, 7am-7pm. Weekend requests confirmed by text within one business hour.
- By appointment Monday-Saturday. We confirm your requested time before arrival.

Rule: do not just list raw hours. Add one line that explains what the customer should expect next.
  • Best for businesses that need a clean first-pass hours block before getting niche-specific.
  • Use this when the profile shows open times but does not reduce scheduling friction or uncertainty.
Holiday and special-hours clarity

Holiday-hours template

Formula:
[Holiday / date] + [open, closed, or limited hours] + [how urgent requests are handled].

Example:
Memorial Day: Closed for in-person visits. Urgent service requests submitted today will be confirmed Tuesday morning.

If you are open limited hours, say the exact window and whether callbacks continue after the office closes.
  • Useful when the business changes hours around holidays, events, or staff shortages.
  • Pairs well with the GBP post prompt library when the operator wants a matching public announcement.

Decision rule

How to choose the right hours wording

Use plain regular hours when the service path is straightforward.
Use “by appointment” only when the business truly confirms times manually.
Use a holiday/special-hours note whenever the listed schedule changes.
Use an after-hours note when requests can still be submitted outside open times.

If the hours block does not explain what happens next, buyers assume slower response and lower reliability.

How should a buyer use these templates?

Pick the closest niche example, swap in the real days, hours, and response pattern, and make sure the wording matches reality. The goal is not sounding polished. The goal is making availability feel current and dependable.
