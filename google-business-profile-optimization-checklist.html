Free Google Business Profile optimization checklist

Use this checklist as a practical review sheet, not a perfection trap. The goal is to spot the profile issues that lower trust, weaken map-pack clicks, or make the next step harder than it should be.

Foundation

Core profile accuracy checklist

Check these first:
- Business name matches the real-world brand exactly.
- Primary category reflects the main revenue service.
- Phone, website, and booking path all work.
- Service area or address setup matches how the business actually operates.
- Core hours, holiday hours, and after-hours expectations are current.

If the profile basics are wrong, everything else compounds the wrong signal.
Positioning

Description + services checklist

Review the profile copy for:
- A clear business description with the actual buyer problem.
- Service names a real buyer would recognize quickly.
- Service descriptions that explain the outcome, not just the label.
- Cities-served wording that is readable instead of stuffed.
- A clear match between categories, services, and top landing-page intent.

If the profile sounds generic, buyers assume the business is generic.
Trust signals

Reviews + reply workflow checklist

Ask:
- Is there an active review request habit after completed jobs?
- Do recent reviews mention the actual services sold?
- Are negative or mixed reviews answered calmly and specifically?
- Does the owner reply in a way that helps future buyers trust the business?
- Is there a repeatable script for asking, following up, and replying?

Weak review flow usually shows up before ranking wins matter.
Proof

Photos + media checklist

Look for:
- Recent team, jobsite, product, before-and-after, or facility photos.
- At least one image type that proves the service result.
- Photo captions or internal organization that match the main services.
- No stale, low-quality, or obviously irrelevant images dominating the profile.
- A simple monthly plan for adding new visual proof.

If the profile lacks proof, the buyer has to imagine the quality alone.
Conversion

Booking path checklist

Check whether:
- The primary CTA makes the next step obvious.
- Messaging, booking, call, or quote language fits the buying motion.
- The products/offers section gives a clear reason to act now.
- Emergency, consult, estimate, or recurring-service paths are easy to identify.
- The website or booking destination matches the promise made inside GBP.

If the profile creates uncertainty at the point of action, visibility alone will not close.
Freshness

Posts + updates checklist

Review:
- Is there any recent posting activity?
- Do posts reinforce current services, offers, or seasonal demand?
- Are updates written in buyer language instead of generic announcements?
- Is there a repeatable weekly or monthly posting rhythm?
- Do offers, photos, and posts feel like one coherent story?

A stale profile can look abandoned even when the business is active.
Plumbers + HVAC

Urgent-service checklist

For emergency or repair-first trades, verify:
- Same-day, urgent, or diagnostic services are visible.
- Hours and after-hours expectations are unambiguous.
- Review language supports speed, professionalism, and resolution.
- Products/offers point to inspections, tune-ups, or repair-first next steps.
- Photos prove field work, trucks, installs, or repair outcomes.

This matters when buyers pick from the map pack fast.
Dentists + med spas

Consult-booking checklist

For appointment-driven categories, verify:
- Categories and services match the actual consult types sold.
- The description sets expectations for new patients or new clients.
- Reviews mention bedside manner, comfort, outcomes, or staff trust.
- Photos support premium positioning and reduce uncertainty.
- Booking CTA language feels calm, clear, and high-trust.

High-intent appointment buyers often judge credibility before they call.
Roofers + movers + cleaners

Estimate-request checklist

For estimate-driven categories, verify:
- The profile makes inspection or quote paths obvious.
- Service areas align with where crews actually work.
- Products/offers support seasonal pushes, first visits, or package framing.
- Messaging and Q&A reduce uncertainty about timing, scope, or availability.
- Photos show jobs big enough to justify trust before contact.

If quote intent is high but the profile feels vague, buyers keep scrolling.
