Free review-gating alternative template library

The goal is simple: ask every real customer for honest feedback, make the review path easy, and handle recovery privately when a customer needs help first. Use these as starting points, not legal advice or a replacement for reading Google’s current policies.

Universal safe ask

Neutral review request text

Hi [First Name] — thank you again for choosing [Business Name]. We appreciate the opportunity to help.

If you are open to it, would you mind sharing honest feedback in a Google review here?

[Google Review Link]

Thank you — it helps other local customers know what to expect.
  • Ask every completed customer, not just the obviously happy ones.
  • Use “honest feedback” or “share your experience” instead of promising only positive-review language.
Universal service recovery

Private follow-up before the public review ask

Hi [First Name] — thank you again for working with [Business Name]. We want to make sure everything landed the way it should.

If there is anything we still need to fix, reply here or call us at [Phone Number] and we will make it right as quickly as we can.

If everything looks good, you can also share your experience here:
[Google Review Link]
  • Do not hide the review link from customers who may be unhappy.
  • Give a real recovery path so the business can resolve issues quickly instead of silently filtering them away.

What is review gating in plain English?

Review gating usually means screening customers first and only pushing obviously happy ones toward the public review link while diverting unhappy customers elsewhere. This page is built to rank for that pain point while giving a safer alternative: ask broadly, keep the wording neutral, and make recovery available to everyone.

What wording should a buyer avoid?

Avoid phrases like “leave us a 5-star review,” “only leave a review if you loved the service,” or any flow that hides the review link unless the customer gives positive private feedback first. Neutral asks like “share your experience” or “leave honest feedback” are cleaner starting points.
